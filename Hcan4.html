To apply for the World Expo, candidates must submit their applications between 6 and 9 years before the proposed opening date of the Expo. Applications to specialised Expo exhibitions require candidates to submit their applications between 5 and 6 years before the opening date. Upon receipt of a single nomination, the Bureau International des Expositions (BIE) begins a 6-month countdown during which all other countries wishing to organize the same exhibition apply to notify the Bureau International des Expositions of their candidacy.



Project Campaign Survey Mission voting
In order to garner support for the Expo project, candidate countries conduct international campaigns once they submit their cities' nomination to host the Expo. Survey missions are organized in each candidate city and include one of the BIE Vice-Presidents, one or more BIE Member States or experts, as well as the BIE Secretary General. At the end of the project screening phase, BIE Member States vote, based on the principle of "one vote per country", by secret ballot during the Bureau General Assembly, to elect the country that will host the Expo.
